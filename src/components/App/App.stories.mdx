# App concept

An app is built up of three main building blocks.

1. Header
2. Sidebar
3. content

## AppHeader

The header is used to identify the name of the app + its owner (or current user). 

<Canvas>
    <AppHeader title="App" subtitle="Owner" />
</Canvas>

Depending on its configuration, the header can be used to toggle the apps menu:

<Canvas>
    <AppHeader title="App" subtitle="Owner" menu={{expanded: false}} />
</Canvas>

... search:

<Canvas>
    <AppHeader title="App" subtitle="Owner" search={{expanded: false}} />
</Canvas>

... or both:

<Canvas>
    <AppHeader title="App" subtitle="Owner" menu={{expanded: false}} search={{expanded: false}} />
</Canvas>

When the search is expanded it will replace the identifier:

<Canvas>
    <AppSearch placeholder="Search app"  />
</Canvas>

The headers can also represent a subview, always display an escape route (back button).

<Canvas>
    <AppSubview title="Uploading" description="3 of 4 images uploaded"  />
</Canvas>


## AppSidebar

The sidebar contains the apps main navigation, primaryAction and menu:

<Canvas>
    <AppSidebar primaryAction={{title: "Create"}} menu={[{title: "Menu 1"}, {title: "Menu 2"}]}  />
</Canvas>


## AppLayout

AppLayout is a container providing the header and sidebar:

<Canvas>
    <div style={{position: "relative", minHeight: 480}}>
        <AppLayout sidebar={{expanded: true}} primaryAction={{title: "Create"}} menu={[{title: "Menu 1"}, {title: "Menu 2"}]}>
            The content of the app, usually a Finder and a View.
        </AppLayout>
    </div>
</Canvas>


## The Finder and views

The Finder will be the main screen for most apps, providing concept for:

1. Creating new content
2. Search
3. Navigating content structures
